Laws and Ethics
Information Security

Group-3

Definitions

Law

It refers to the body of legal rules, codes, and standards that govern the protection of information and information systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It encompasses a wide range of legal principles, including, Privacy laws, Data protection laws, Cybersecurity laws, Intellectual property laws, and Contract law.

Ethics

It refers to the moral principles that guide the behavior of individuals and organizations in the field of information technology. It involves making choices about right and wrong, good and bad, in the context of information security.

Key Differences

Aspect Law Ethics
Laws are formal, enforceable mandates from governing bodies, such as penalties for data breaches under the Cybercrime Prevention Act. Ethics are informal moral principles influenced by societal values and culture, guiding behavior beyond legal compliance. For instance, ethically, an IT professional might disclose vulnerabilities responsibly, even without legal obligation.
AUTHORITY Laws are imposed by a governing body ethics are shaped by societal values and personal morality.
SUNCTIONS Violating laws results in legal penalties violating ethical norms may lead to social disapproval or loss of professional reputation

Example

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

A regulation in the European Union designed to protect individuals' personal data and privacy, requiring organizations to handle data transparently, securely, and responsibly.

Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)

A U.S. law that ensures the privacy and security of healthcare information, setting standards for handling protected health information (PHI).

Privacy (Data Minimization)

Ensures that only the necessary amount of personal data is collected and processed, reducing the risk of misuse and overreach.

Security (Confidentiality)

Focuses on protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access, ensuring that data remains private and secure.
